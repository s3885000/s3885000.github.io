Cybersecurity

What is Cybersecurity?

In this 4.0 World, thanks to the rapid earth-shattering increment of convenience, operation, and reliance on the Internet. We can now purchase goods online, pay bills, and do all sorts of things virtually without the need for physical activities. And because of that, the Internet can act like "a double-edged sword." It can be a benefit for you at the same time it can be bad for you. With such a vast population worldwide, who has access to the Internet can be a vulnerable place for innocent people to get attack anonymously, just like a real-life crime. The Internet called them hackers who go after people's sensitive information without the victim's consent. Thanks to the technologies we are currently having right now Cybersecurity is here to protect people's privacy from getting exposed.

*image was taken from the Internet

Cybersecurity is the process of protecting networks, computer data, sensitive information against malicious entry or illegal use, and the process of maintaining confidentiality, integrity, and access to information. Cybersecurity requires methods to protect data and operational infrastructure from unwanted digital access to separate networks' digital elements. Depending on the type of network you are connected to and the kind of cyber threats to which you are vulnerable, there are various ways to integrate Cybersecurity.

  • Malware attacks

    • Malware attacks such as trojans, adware, spyware, to name a few, are some of the most common examples of cyber-attacks. Your operating system may have been compromised by such malware viruses embedded inside the attachments once the victim has opened the unusual attachments online.

  • Phishing attacks

    • The sort of cyber-attack where an experienced hacker tries to deliver fake emails that seem to come from a reputable source is another common cyber-attack called a phishing attack. This is done to install Malware or steal data such as credit card information and login credentials sensitive.

  • Man-in-the-middle attack (MITM)

    • From here, the hacker gets access to the data path between the computer of the victim and the server of the website. By doing so, the hacker's device takes over an IP address, remotely intercepting the contact line between the victim and the website. With unsecured internet networks and even by Malware, this typically exists.

    *image was taken from the Internet

    To deter potential cyber threats and enforce a few cyber protection practices, citizens should also create a firewall. The name implies a virtual shield between a user's machine and the Internet. Firewalls filter incoming and outgoing data from the user interface to secure the network and can be used either for software applications or hardware reinforcements.
